kergothsnouto: image != distro. the image just selects packages, it doesn't control what package manager is in use, or other aspects of distribution policy. in oe/yocto, we have three independent, orthogonal axes. machine, distro, and image. in general, any combination of machine, distro, and image should work.20:46
kergothbeyond that, the package manager isn't what makes centos centos. there are tons of small decisions made by distribituion maintainers that makes that distro that distro. switching your distro for your embedded device to use rpm/yum won't make it centos. it might be more familiar to users of centos, however20:47
kergothyou can select ipk, deb, or rpm packaging, which one you use is up to you. poky defaults to rpm, angstrom and others default to ipk, it varies20:48
kergothwhen it comes to overall structure and configuration files, most of the oe/yocto based distros are more debian in feel than redhat/fedora/centos, i.e. use of update-rc.d rather than chkconfig for sysvinit startup script control, the existence and use of update-alternatives, and the default package naming based on library soname (i.e. libc6 rather than glibc)20:49
