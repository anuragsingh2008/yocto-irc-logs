bluelightningseveral people have asked why we don't do that out of the box13:29
bluelightningI don't know that there has been a satisfactory answer13:29
frayanswer is that many of our systems simply don't ever use ld.so.cache..13:30
fraysecondary reason -- so far it's VERY rare that you (as the -system- developer need to ever put an entry into ld.so.conf (let alone create ld.so.conf.d/*.conf entries)13:31
fraywe want to actually discourage that behavior -- as it usually means broken system items..13:31
fray(ld.so.cache is often used as a crutch for broken libraries that do not have proper filename/sonames... so the RTLD (ld.so) can't find them w/o the cache)13:32
fraythe other piece is for libraries installed into non-standard locations..  but they shouldn't be cached, because the point of non-standard locations is to keep them outside of the regular loading namespace..13:32
frayso if you suddenly cache them -- they are now availabel globally..13:32
fray"just say no"13:32
fray(another way to look at it -- if the system requires ldconfig to have run -- you have a broken system..  if you are running it as a performance improvement.. GREAT!  that is what it is there for)13:33
